# SPIA Viet Nam Report Research Baseline

## 1. Source

The project will use the uploaded report as a core research baseline:

**SPIA Viet Nam Report: Global Ambitions, Sustainable Pathways**  
Standing Panel on Impact Assessment / CGIAR-related country study for Viet Nam.

This report should guide CSAV-DP across planning, KPI discovery, source inventory, Gold mart design, advisory logic, and evidence practices.

## 2. How CSAV-DP Will Use This Research

The report is accepted as a **domain and evidence baseline**, not as automatically verified operational data.

It will be used to guide:

- climate-smart agriculture problem framing;
- candidate KPI and signal definitions;
- CS-MAP-based risk/adaptation logic;
- recommended adaptation option categories;
- candidate source dependencies such as VHLSS, province-level agricultural plans, CS-MAPs, ACBs, administrative geography, weather/climate, salinity, and rice/crop practice sources;
- evidence expectations for source assessment and KPI feasibility;
- limitations and caveats around measuring farmer adoption, climate services, and advisory reach.

## 3. High-Value Concepts for CSAV-DP

Important concepts extracted from the report for project design:

1. **CS-MAP / Climate-Smart Mapping and Adaptation Planning**
   - Risk maps and adaptation plans are location-, risk-, and season-specific.
   - They connect climate risk with adaptation options such as planting date changes, crop shifting, cropping pattern changes, and no rice cultivation in high-risk situations.

2. **Risk levels**
   - CS-MAPs classify risk using categories such as no/minimal risk, low risk, medium risk, and high risk.
   - Risk definitions vary by region and risk type, such as drought, salinity, flooding, rainfall, reservoir water levels, and expected productivity loss.

3. **Adaptation recommendations**
   - Examples include changing planting dates, changing cropping patterns, shifting from rice to other crops, or not planting rice in certain high-risk seasons/areas.
   - These are strong candidates for system-generated advisory output categories in CSAV-DP.

4. **VHLSS and measurement methods**
   - The report uses VHLSS modules, household-level data, commune-level data, province-level plans, geospatial matching, content analysis, and adoption estimates.
   - This provides a useful model for how CSAV-DP should document source evidence, measurement caveats, and feasibility.

5. **Evidence-bounded advisory logic**
   - The project should not make unsupported farmer advice.
   - Advisory outputs must be traceable to validated data, rules, risk levels, and documented caveats.

## 4. Implications for KPI Discovery

KPI Discovery should be organized around four groups:

1. **Monitoring KPIs**
   - Which areas are at high climate/agricultural risk?
   - Which areas should be prioritized first?

2. **Explanation signals**
   - Which factor contributes to the risk: drought, salinity, flooding, reservoir water level, rainfall anomaly, crop season, planting date, or geographic exposure?

3. **Advisory outputs**
   - What recommendation category should be generated for a location/season/risk level?
   - Examples: adjust planting date, shift crop, change cropping pattern, avoid rice cultivation, monitor only.

4. **Data quality KPIs**
   - Is the data fresh, complete, traceable, and good enough to generate a dashboard or advisory record?

## 5. Guardrails

The report is not enough by itself to finalize implementation.

Before using any KPI, source, rule, or advisory logic as final, CSAV-DP must still validate:

- data availability;
- source license;
- source grain;
- field existence;
- temporal coverage;
- geographic coverage;
- schema stability;
- data quality;
- transformation assumptions;
- rule applicability to the selected MVP domain slice.

**Decision:** Use the SPIA Viet Nam Report as the project-wide research baseline.
