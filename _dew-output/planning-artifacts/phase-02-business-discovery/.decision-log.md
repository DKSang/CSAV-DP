## Step 1 — Consumer Priority

**Question:** In the first MVP, which consumer is primary?

**Decision:** A. Data analyst is the primary consumer; farmer/end user is a downstream/end-user constraint.

**Interpretation:** The first implementation should optimize for analyst needs: reliable Gold marts, documented risk logic, quality evidence, and dashboard-ready outputs. Farmer-facing web/app advisory features remain part of the product direction, but should be built after the analytical data foundation is trustworthy.

**Status:** Confirmed by user.

**Date:** 2026-05-26

## Step 2 — Analyst Role & Decision Workflow

**Question:** What will the data analyst use this data product for?

**Decision:** B and C.

- **B. Analyze risk areas** — compare province/ward/region to identify locations needing attention.
- **C. Prepare insights for decision-makers or farmer-facing team** — translate risk analysis into interpretable insights or advisory inputs.

**Interpretation:** The analyst workflow should move from risk-area identification to insight preparation. The data product must therefore support both comparative risk analysis and explainable outputs that can be reused in dashboard or farmer-facing advisory features.

**Status:** Confirmed by user.

**Date:** 2026-05-26

## Step 3 — Core Business Questions

**Question:** What central business questions should the data product answer?

**Decision:** A, B, C, and D.

- **A. Which areas currently have the highest climate/agricultural risk over time?**
- **B. Which areas should be prioritized for warning or intervention first?**
- **C. Which factors contribute to risk: weather, seasonality, location, salinity, or other data?**
- **D. What action recommendation should farmers in each area receive?**

**Interpretation for MVP:** To keep the MVP feasible, these questions should be layered by priority:

1. **Core monitoring questions:** A and B.
2. **Explanation question:** C, used to explain why an area is risky.
3. **Advisory question:** D, treated as evidence-bounded advisory logic, not production-grade advice.

The Gold data mart should therefore preserve enough dimensional context to support monitoring, prioritization, explanation, and later advisory outputs.

**Status:** Confirmed by user.

**Date:** 2026-05-26

## Step 4 — Action Taken After Answer

**Question:** When the analyst answers the business questions, what action happens next?

**Decision:** A and C.

- **A. Create dashboard/report warning about risk areas** — convert risk monitoring and prioritization into a visible analyst-facing output.
- **C. Create recommendation content for a farmer-facing web app** — transform explained risk signals into simplified advisory content for downstream users.

**Interpretation:** The business workflow should move from Gold mart analysis to dashboard/report outputs, then to farmer-facing advisory content. The web app should not generate unsupported advice directly; it should consume documented, evidence-bounded outputs from the analytical layer.

**Status:** Confirmed by user.

**Date:** 2026-05-26
