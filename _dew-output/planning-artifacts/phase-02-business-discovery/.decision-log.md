## Step 1 — Consumer Priority

**Question:** In the first MVP, which consumer is primary?

**Decision:** A. Data analyst is the primary consumer for analytical consumption; farmer/end user is the downstream product consumer.

**Interpretation:** The first implementation should optimize for analyst needs: reliable Gold marts, documented risk logic, quality evidence, and dashboard-ready outputs. Farmer-facing web/app advisory features remain a core downstream product direction, but they should consume system-generated advisory outputs rather than depend on analysts manually preparing every recommendation.

**Status:** Confirmed by user.

**Date:** 2026-05-26

## Step 2 — Analyst Role & Decision Workflow

**Question:** What will the data analyst use this data product for?

**Revised Decision:** B, with C removed from analyst responsibility.

- **B. Analyze risk areas** — compare province/ward/region to identify locations needing attention.
- **Dashboard/report creation** — analyst uses the data product to create or consume dashboard/report outputs for risk monitoring.

**Not analyst responsibility:** The analyst should not be required to manually create farmer-facing recommendation content every time new data arrives. That would slow down the web app workflow and create a human bottleneck.

**Interpretation:** The analyst workflow should focus on risk-area analysis, validation, interpretation, and dashboard/report usage. Farmer-facing advisory content should be generated by the system from validated Gold marts plus explicit, versioned, evidence-bounded advisory rules.

**Status:** Revised by user correction.

**Date:** 2026-05-26

## Step 3 — Core Business Questions

**Question:** What central business questions should the data product answer?

**Decision:** A, B, C, and D.

- **A. Which areas currently have the highest climate/agricultural risk over time?**
- **B. Which areas should be prioritized for warning or intervention first?**
- **C. Which factors contribute to risk: weather, seasonality, location, salinity, or other data?**
- **D. What action recommendation should farmers in each area receive?**

**Interpretation for MVP:** To keep the MVP feasible, these questions should be layered by priority:

1. **Core monitoring questions:** A and B.
2. **Explanation question:** C, used to explain why an area is risky.
3. **System-generated advisory question:** D, produced by the project/system from validated data and rules, not manually prepared by analysts for every data refresh.

The Gold data mart should therefore preserve enough dimensional context to support monitoring, prioritization, explanation, and later advisory outputs.

**Status:** Confirmed by user, with advisory ownership clarified.

**Date:** 2026-05-26

## Step 4 — Action Taken After Answer

**Question:** When the analyst answers the business questions, what action happens next?

**Revised Decision:** A for analyst action, plus system-owned C for product action.

- **A. Analyst creates or uses dashboard/report warning about risk areas** — convert risk monitoring and prioritization into a visible analyst-facing output.
- **C. System creates recommendation content for a farmer-facing web app** — transform explained risk signals into simplified advisory content through rules/logic owned by the project, not manual analyst work.

**Interpretation:** The business workflow should move from Gold mart and risk logic to two serving outputs:

1. **Analyst-facing output:** dashboard/report for monitoring and analysis.
2. **Farmer-facing output:** web app feature that consumes system-generated advisory records or recommendation categories.

The web app should not wait for a data analyst to manually prepare recommendation content after every data refresh. It should consume documented, evidence-bounded, reproducible outputs from the analytical/advisory layer.

**Status:** Revised by user correction.

**Date:** 2026-05-26

**Question:** What trust expectation should guide the data product?

**Decision:** C. Trusted shared product with quality checks.

**Interpretation:** The project should behave like a trusted shared analytical product for portfolio demonstration: quality checks, documented assumptions, data lineage, source caveats, and reproducible evidence are required.
