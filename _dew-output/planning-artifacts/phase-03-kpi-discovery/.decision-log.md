## Step 1 — KPI Scope Group

**Question:** Which KPI groups should be included in the first KPI Discovery round?

**Decision:** A. Use all four KPI groups in priority order, based on the SPIA Viet Nam Report.

Priority order:

1. **Monitoring KPIs** — identify and rank climate/agricultural risk by location and time.
2. **Explanation signals** — explain which factors contribute to risk, such as drought, salinity, flooding, rainfall, reservoir water level, crop season, planting date, or geographic exposure.
3. **Advisory outputs** — generate evidence-bounded recommendation categories/actions for the farmer-facing web app.
4. **Data quality KPIs** — measure freshness, completeness, traceability, and quality status of data and advisory outputs.

**Interpretation:** KPI Discovery will not invent generic KPIs. It will derive candidate KPI hypotheses from the SPIA Viet Nam Report, especially CS-MAP, adaptation planning, VHLSS measurement logic, province-level agricultural plans, risk levels, and recommended adaptation options.

## Step 2 — Primary Monitoring KPI Definition

**Question:** Which monitoring KPI should be defined first?

**Decision:** A. Climate-Agricultural Risk Level.

**Candidate KPI ID:** `KPI-MON-001`

**KPI name:** Climate-Agricultural Risk Level

**Business question:** Which areas currently have the highest climate/agricultural risk over time?

**Decision supported:** Identify and rank locations that require monitoring, warning, or downstream advisory generation.

**Candidate formula / logic:**

Assign each location-season-scenario record to a categorical risk level:

```text
risk_level ∈ {no_or_minimal_risk, low_risk, medium_risk, high_risk}
```

The classification logic should follow validated CS-MAP/risk-map definitions where available, such as drought, salinity, flooding, rainfall, reservoir water level, or expected productivity-loss thresholds.

**Candidate grain:**

```text
location × season × scenario_or_forecast_period × risk_type
```

Potential location levels:

- province;
- district if historical source uses it;
- ward/commune if available and compatible with current administrative structure;
- mapped risk polygon if GIS data is available.

**Candidate dimensions:**

- province;
- ward/commune or mapped polygon;
- region/agroecological region;
- season;
- crop;
- risk type;
- scenario/forecast condition;
- time period/year;
- source version.

**Required fields — candidate only:**

- location identifier or geometry;
- season/cropping period;
- risk type;
- risk category or numeric threshold input;
- scenario or forecast condition;
- crop or farming system;
- source timestamp/version;
- quality/status fields.

**Candidate source dependencies — not verified:**

- CS-MAP / climate-smart risk maps;
- province-level agricultural plans;
- weather/climate forecast or historical climate source;
- salinity/flooding/reservoir/rainfall indicators where available;
- Vietnam administrative geography dataset;
- SPIA report as research baseline.

**Feasibility status:** Hypothesis — not verified.

**Caveat:** This KPI must not be marked as feasible until source files, fields, geospatial grain, temporal grain, and risk-level definitions are validated in later phases.

**Status:** Confirmed by user as primary monitoring KPI.

**Date:** 2026-05-26
